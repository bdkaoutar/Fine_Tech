Questions about Blockchain & Smart Contracts

1. What is a smart contract and how does it work on the Ethereum blockchain?
A smart contract is a self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement directly written into lines of code. On the Ethereum blockchain, smart contracts are deployed and executed on the network, enabling decentralized, transparent, and secure transactions without intermediaries.
2. How does Ethereum ensure secure transactions?
Ethereum uses cryptography and consensus mechanisms like Proof of Stake (PoS) to secure transactions. Each transaction is validated by the network participants, making it immutable and tamper-proof once confirmed.
3. What is the role of tokens in Ethereum-based smart contracts?
Tokens in Ethereum can represent assets, ownership, or value. They can be used in decentralized applications (dApps) to facilitate transactions, create new financial instruments, and represent real-world assets on the blockchain.
4. What is decentralized finance (DeFi) on Ethereum?
Decentralized finance (DeFi) is a movement that uses blockchain technology, primarily Ethereum, to recreate traditional financial systems like lending, borrowing, and trading in a decentralized, open-source, and permissionless way, without the need for intermediaries.
5. How are Ethereum transactions validated?
Ethereum transactions are validated by the network through consensus mechanisms like Proof of Stake (PoS), where validators verify transactions and add them to the blockchain. This process ensures security and decentralization.
